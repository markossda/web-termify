What is Data Plane Segregation?

Data Plane Segregation The separation of the data forwarding path from management and control planes within network infrastructure to improve security and reduce risk of compromise. Defined in NIST SP 800-207 and IETF RFC 7426.

Source: ISO 27001, NIST Cybersecurity Framework, MITRE ATT&CK

How is “Data Plane Segregation” Used in Practice?

Implement data plane segregation to prevent unauthorized access from control or management networks and to contain potential breaches within the forwarding path.
